1957

i) October 4- The Soviet Union launched the first satellite, Sputnik, into space.

ii) November 3- The Soviet spacecraft Sputnik 2 was launched with a dog named Laika on board. Laika did not survive the voyage.

1958

i) January 31- Explorer 1 was the first satellite launched by the union states when it was sent into orbit on January 31, 1958. It was designed and built by the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) of the California Institute of Technology. The satellite was sent aloft from Cape Canaveral in Florida by the Jupiter C rocket that was designed, built, and launched by the Army Ballistic Missile Agency (ABMA) under the direction of Dr. Wernher Von Braun.

1961

i) April 12- Russian cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin became the first human in space.

ii) May 5- Astronaut Alan Shepard became the first American in space.

iii) May 25- President Kennedy challenged the country to put a man on the moon by the end of the decade.

1966

i) February 3- The Russian spacecraft Luna 9 became the first spacecraft to land on the moon.

ii) June 2- Surveyor 1 became the first American spacecraft to land on the moon.

1969

i) July 20- Neil Armstrong and "Buzz" Aldrin became the first men on the moon.

1970

i) April 11- Apollo 13 was launched.

ii) September 12- The Soviet craft Luna 16 was launched and became the first automatic spacecraft to return soil samples of the moon.

iii) November 17- The Soviet automatic robot Lunokhod 1 landed on the moon with Luna 17.

iv) December 11-17- The Soviet Venera 7 became the first probe to land on Venus.

1976

i) September- The American probe Viking discovered water frost on the Martian surface.

1981

i) April 12- Columbia became the first Space Shuttle to be launched.

ii) August 26- Voyager 2 reached Saturn and began transmitting images.

2003

i) February 1- The Space Shuttle Columbia broke up on re-entry into the Earth's atmosphere.

ii) August 25- NASA launched the largest-diameter infrared telescope ever in space, The Spitzer Space Telescope.

iii) September 21- NASA's Galileo mission ended a 14-year exploration of the solar system's largest planet and its moons with the spacecraft crashing by design into Jupiter at 108,000 mph.

2009

i) March 6- The NASA spacecraft Kepler was launched. Its mission is to search for planets outside our solar system, in a distant area of the Milky Way.

ii) June 18- NASA launched the LUNAR crater observation and sensing satellite, also known as LCROSS. The mission is to confirm the presence or absence of ice on the moon. On November 13, 2009, NASA scientists announced the discovery of a "significant amount" of ice in a crater near the moon's south pole.

2012

i) May 22- SpaceX, a commercial space company, launched its Dragon C2+ mission to resupply the International Space Station.

ii) August 6- NASA's Curiosity rover successfully landed on Mars. As large as a car, it carried an array of advanced new instruments and experiments.

2016

i) August 28- ISRO Scramjet Engine Technology Demonstrator.

ii) August 26- Voyager 2 reached Saturn and began transmitting images.

2019

i) January 3- First soft landing on the far side of the Moon. First germination of seeds on another celestial body.

ii) April 10- First direct photograph of a black hole and its vicinity.

2021

i) April 20- First production of oxygen on another celestial body (Mars).

ii) December 14- First spacecraft to fly through the atmosphere of a star (the Sun's corona).

iii) December 25- Launch of the largest space telescope to date.

2022

i) September 26- First spacecraft to make contact with an asteroid moon (impact on Dimorphos).

2024

i) March 14- First successful demonstration of in-space propellant transfer.

ii) June 6- First successful instance of both stages of a launch vehicle returned for a controlled landing.

iii) June 25- First samples returned from the far side of the Moon.
